=== TWEET 1 ===
I've spent two months building something I wish existed. Project HOLLOW: a member-hosted, E2EE Discord alternative.

No company owns your server. Your group does. Every message is cryptographically signed, so nobody can ever fake a screenshot of you. 🧵

=== TWEET 2 ===
Every chat app has the same flaw. Discord owns your servers, friends, memories, communities… gone the moment they decide to delete it (or ban you for no reason). Plus, anyone can open Photoshop and "prove" you said something you didn't.

=== TWEET 3 ===
What if no company or even owner hosted the server? What if your friends did? Collectively, fully encrypted and automatic? In HOLLOW, messages sync peer-to-peer. If anyone is online, the server is alive. No admin panel or data center to seize.

=== TWEET 4 ===
I'm proud of the signature system. Every message you send is signed with your key. There's a built-in tool to verify any message. Screenshots won't pass. The truth is provable!

=== TWEET 5 ===
What works today: text, voice, video, screen share, file sharing, DMs, friends, servers etc.
Native desktop app built on Flutter+Rust (no Electron/Web). E2EE everywhere (MLS for groups, Olm for DMs, SFrame for calls).
Local storage encrypted with SQLCipher.
No blockchains/tokens.
